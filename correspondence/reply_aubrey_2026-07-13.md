Here's the difference. A centriole's function is geometric, not biochemical. Damaged collagen or crystallin loses mechanical or optical function. But a damaged centriole — as the basal body of the primary cilium — distorts Hedgehog, Wnt, and PDGFRα signalling geometry across decades of post-mitotic life. You can't ubiquitinate and replace a centriole piecemeal without dismantling the entire 9-fold template. The oocyte doesn't wait for slow turnover; it eliminates the old centriole and builds one de novo. That's the only rejuvenation event mammals actually perform. The question is whether we can borrow it.
