# CONCEPT — ERR

## Eliminate, Reprogram, Rebuild

The centriole is a geometric template. ALMS1 condensates seed CEP152-CEP63-PCNT aggregates, which template cartwheel formation (Ozaki & Tsou, 2026). SAS-6 ring assembly is surface-guided — the mother's surface properties determine the daughter's cartwheel geometry (Banterle et al., 2021). A damaged surface assembles a damaged seed, which templates a damaged cartwheel.

Three counters of replicative aging can be reset: telomeres, mitochondria, the epigenome. The fourth — the centriole — has never been reset in a somatic cell.

**ERR proposes to do exactly that.**

### The experiment

1. **Eliminate** — PLK4 inhibition (centrinone, 100 nM, 48h) removes centrioles. Verify by UExM (<5% CP110+).
2. **Reprogram** — DUX4 + TPRX1 mRNA transfection. These factors activate the zygotic genome and drive totipotency (8C-like state).
3. **Rebuild** — Withdraw centrinone. Monitor de novo centriole assembly by SAS-6::GFP.

### Why this matters

Every oocyte eliminates its centrioles before fertilization. The sperm brings only seeds — not templates. Nature clears the slate. If we can do this in a somatic cell, we remove the fourth counter.

### Open questions

- Does the mother centriole surface degrade with age? (Cryo-ET — not yet done)
- Does a new centriole built in old cytoplasm inherit old markers?
- Does DUX4-induced totipotency survive centriole loss?

**Status:** Hypothesis. Strong inference, but inference.
